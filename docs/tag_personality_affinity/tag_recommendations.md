Tag,Recommendation,Implementation Example
Written / Async,Adopt asynchronous and text-based methods to reduce social pressure and allow cognitive time.,"Release newsletters, recorded video demos, full agendas written in advance."
Verbal-Heavy,Increase bandwidth through direct oral exchanges to unblock complex situations.,"Ad-hoc calls (rather than long message threads), daily rituals, virtual or physical coffee chats."
Visual / Tactile,Materialize the workflow through visual aids to facilitate a shared understanding.,"Color-coded Kanban boards, progression diagrams (R/A/G), interactive User Story Mapping."
Co-located / On-Site,Promote physical presence to maximize the richness of interactions and resolve latent bottlenecks.,"Common office days, design workshops on physical whiteboards."
Small Group / Pair,Restrict interactions to small groups to ensure psychological safety and skill transfer.,"Pair Programming, 'Three Amigos' format (Dev/QA/PO), targeted feature sub-groups."
Whole Crowd,Involve all stakeholders to ensure global alignment and break down silos.,"Cross-team Sprint Planning, town halls (PI Planning), department-wide retrospectives."
Solo-Capable,Secure periods of autonomy to reach a 'flow' state without synchronous dependency.,"Focus Protected Time slots, right to disconnect, end-to-end isolated ticket assignments."
Structured / Facilitated,Enforce a rigorous procedural framework with a facilitator to avoid political debates.,"Strict retrospective canvases, neutral facilitator, rigorous preparation of inputs/outputs."
Spontaneous / Improv,Loosen the framework to give autonomy back to experts and react quickly to the unexpected.,"Natural planning adjustments between developers, justified technical shortcuts during crises."
Public / High Visibility,Ensure formal exposure of work to drive accountability and highlight successes.,"Sprint Reviews with stakeholders, cross-department technical presentations."
Consensus-Driven,Require explicit collective alignment to ensure consistent quality and avoid hidden debt.,"Strict application of DoD/DoR, cross-validation of architectural choices by seniors."
Critical / Introspective,Establish moments of factual critical analysis to aim for excellence and continuous improvement.,"Systematic asynchronous Code Reviews, blameless technical post-mortems after incidents."
Time-Boxed,Strictly limit allocated time to prevent stalling and maintain high engagement.,"Dailies limited to 10 min with a timer, time-boxing exploratory technical debates (Spikes)."
Gamified,Depersonalize debates through game mechanics to streamline decision-making.,"Planning Poker, T-shirt sizing estimation, gamification of retrospectives."
Role-Fluid,Establish a rotation of responsibilities to prevent burnout or technical erosion.,"Scrum Master role rotation (Round-Robin), shared hosting of Daily Stand-ups."
Fast-Feedback,Shorten the technical feedback loop to avoid frustration related to latency.,"Build time optimization (CI/CD), canary releases, automated local tests before commit."
User-Feedback Oriented,Create a direct channel with the end user to validate the actual utility of the product.,"Preview/Beta environments, frequent micro-releases, customer presence at Sprint Reviews."
Documented / Traceable,Perpetuate information to reduce uncertainty and dependence on knowledge silos.,"Architecture Decision Records (ADR), Wiki documentation, strict traceability in Jira."
Maintenance-Aware,Integrate technical debt management into Sprint capacity to ensure system viability.,"Fixed resource quota for refactoring (e.g. 20% Tech Debt), visible Tech Debt Backlog."
