
# Frameworks & Methods

This section categorizes practices based on the frameworks or methods they belong to. The goal is to help users quickly identify practices relevant to their specific agile or lean approach, while also providing a catch-all category for general facilitation techniques.

Summarized list of categories:

 - Scrum
 - Kanban
 - XP
 - Lean
 - Scaled Agile
 - Product Management
 - Design Thinking & UX
 - Project Management
 - Agile
 - Facilitation & Workshops





## 1. Core Agile & Lean Frameworks (Team-Level)
These frameworks focus on the execution and delivery at the team level.

* **Scrum:** Sprint Planning, Sprint Retrospective, Definition of Done, etc.
* **Kanban:** Visualize Workflow, STATIK, Work-in-Progress (WIP) limits.
* **XP (Extreme Programming):** Test-Driven Development (TDD), Pair Programming, Continuous Integration.
* **Lean:** Value Stream Mapping, 5 Whys, Lean Prioritization.

---

## 2. Scaled Agile Frameworks (Enterprise-Level)

## 3. Product Discovery & Design
**Includes:** Design Thinking, User-Centric Design, Product Management.

## 4. General / Legacy Management
**Includes:** Project Management.

> **Project Management** is used for traditional governance practices such as the RACI Matrix and Stakeholder Mapping.

---

## 5. Generic Agile & Niche Frameworks
**Includes:** Agile, DSDM, Crystal, FDD.

> Consolidate lesser-used methodologies (DSDM, Crystal, FDD) into a general **Agile** umbrella. This serves as a catch-all for practices that are widely used in agility but don't belong to a strict framework (e.g., Team Canvas, T-Shirt Estimation).

---

## 6. Facilitation & Agnostic Practices

> Generic techniques, Liberating Structures (like 1-2-4-All), and voting mechanisms that don't originate from a specific software framework.
